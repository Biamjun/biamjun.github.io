About Me

I am a driven and analytical researcher currently pursuing an **MRes in Machine Learning and Big Data in the Physical Sciences** at Imperial College London. My academic foundation is built on a **BSc in Astrophysics from UCL**, where I developed strong problem-solving skills and a deep understanding of complex data analysis and computational modeling.

I am actively seeking a **PhD position or AI Researcher role** starting in Autumn 2026, where I can apply my skills to advance AI methodology and solve meaningful scientific or industrial challenges.

Research Interest

My passion lies at the intersection of fundamental science and artificial intelligence. I am particularly fascinated by the power of **generative models (GANs, Diffusion)** to solve complex simulation problems that are currently intractable with traditional methods, such as accelerating the massive Monte Carlo simulations used in particle physics.

My goal is to pursue research in **Physics-Informed AI (PIML)**, developing models that not only mimic data but also understand and embed the fundamental physical laws governing a system. I believe this is the key to creating reliable and interpretable AI for scientific discovery.

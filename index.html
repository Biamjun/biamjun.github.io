Research Interest

My core research interest is at the intersection of fundamental science and machine learning. I am driven to develop widely-applicable novel methodologies and application-specific methods that can lead to breakthroughs in real-world problems in the physical sciences and industry. My goal is to pursue research in 'AI for Science', specifically developing models that not only mimic data but also understand and embed the fundamental physical laws governing a system.

My upcoming MRes project, "Machine Learning Meets Particle Physics," will focus on constructing and training a generative neural network to produce very large simulation samples quickly. This directly tackles the limitations of traditional Monte Carlo simulations (Geant4), which are too slow to generate the massive datasets needed to optimize detector design and understand performance. This new method will be applied to the search for physics Beyond the Standard Model by enabling the rigorous simulation of rare processes, such as the muon-to-electron conversion for the COMET experiment. This project is therefore the perfect environment to develop and validate these advanced AI methodologies against a statistically complex, high-stakes scientific problem.

Quantum Metrology for Spacetime Parameter Estimation

(A*STAR Quantum Innovation Centre, Singapore)
Analysed the performance of quantum probe states and thier relative errors for estimating spacetime parameters like the Schwarzschild Radius, Hubble Constant, and Gravitational Wave parameter.

Dec 2024 – Mar 2025

Machine Learning for Neutrino Detection

(UCL)
Developed CNN and LSTM-based models with time-series neutrino data and DFT to predict chirp signal parameters. Achieved MSE of 0.0980 using ensemble learning, demonstrating effective ML for neutrino (quirk) identification.

Oct 2024 – Dec 2024

Far-IR Emission as a tracer for star formation

(UCL)
Analysed observations of dust emission made at 850µm using the SCUBA-2 camera on the James Clerk Maxwell Telescope (JCMT). Used a source finding algorithm to investigate the statistical properties of cores, including determining their masses.

Oct 2024 – Dec 2024

Far-IR spectroscopy of AFGL2688

(UCL)
Conducted data analysis of spectra from the SPIRE Fourier Transform Spectrometer on the Herschel Space Observatory, identifying molecular lines and analyzing the physical properties of the source.
